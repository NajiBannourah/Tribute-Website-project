Martin Luther King Jr.

Civil Rights Leader and Icon of the American Civil Rights Movement

A Look at Martin Luther King Jr.'s Life and Legacy:

  • 1929 - Born Michael King Jr. in Atlanta, Georgia, USA.
  • 1955 - Leads the Montgomery Bus Boycott after the arrest of Rosa Parks, emerging as a prominent leader in the Civil Rights Movement.
  • 1963 - Delivers his famous "I Have a Dream" speech during the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom, advocating for racial equality and an end to discrimination.
  • 1964 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his nonviolent resistance to racial prejudice and his efforts to achieve racial equality through peaceful means.
  • 1965 - Leads the Selma to Montgomery marches, which contributed to the passage of the Voting Rights Act, aimed at eliminating racial discrimination in voting.
  • 1968 - Assassinated in Memphis, Tennessee, at the age of 39, leaving a lasting impact on the Civil Rights Movement and American society.

"Martin Luther King Jr. was a visionary leader who dedicated his life to the pursuit of equality and justice for all Americans. His legacy continues to inspire generations around the world."
